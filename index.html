DBQ Example Breakdown

Prompt

Prompt:
Evaluate the extent to which the development of trade routes affected the societies along those routes during the period 600 to 1450.

Introductory Paragraph

Context: The period from 600 to 1450 saw the development of extensive trade networks across Eurasia, Africa, and the Indian Ocean. The Silk Roads, Trans-Saharan routes, and maritime routes across the Indian Ocean linked distant regions, facilitating the exchange of goods, culture, and ideas. (This section provides background information on the rise of global trade routes.)
Thesis Statement: The development of trade routes from 600 to 1450 had a profound impact on the societies along those routes, fostering economic prosperity, cultural exchange, and religious diffusion. (This is the thesis statement, clearly addressing the prompt and presenting a claim.)
Line of Reasoning: The impacts of the trade routes can be seen in the rise of commercial cities like Baghdad and Venice, the spread of new technologies, and the establishment of new cultural and religious exchanges. (This portion explains how the thesis will be supported.)

Body Paragraphs

Topic Sentence (Economic Impact): The development of trade routes significantly boosted economies by promoting commerce, increasing wealth, and fostering urbanization in key trade cities. (This introduces the first supporting argument.)
Evidence: Cities such as Baghdad, Venice, and Timbuktu flourished as key commercial hubs, drawing merchants and artisans, and benefiting from innovations like banking and credit systems. (Specific historical examples supporting the argument.)
Analysis: These economic developments not only increased wealth but also contributed to cultural and intellectual advancements. (Explains how the evidence supports the thesis.)
Topic Sentence (Cultural Exchange): Trade routes facilitated significant cultural exchanges, allowing for the spread of religions, ideas, and technologies across regions. (Introduces the second key argument.)
Evidence: The Silk Roads enabled the spread of Buddhism into East Asia, while the Indian Ocean trade network helped disseminate Islam into Southeast Asia and East Africa. (Provides relevant historical examples.)
Analysis: These interactions not only expanded religious influence but also shaped the cultural identities of societies along the trade routes. (Explains the impact of the evidence.)
Topic Sentence (Technological Diffusion): Trade routes were instrumental in spreading innovations such as gunpowder, paper-making, and navigational tools, which had lasting effects on societies. (Introduces the third argument.)
Evidence: The Mongol Empire played a key role in transmitting Chinese gunpowder technology to Europe, revolutionizing warfare. (Specific historical detail.)
Analysis: This technological diffusion not only transformed military tactics but also contributed to European state-building and expansion. (Explains the significance of the evidence.)

Conclusion

Restating the Thesis: While trade routes significantly transformed many societies by fostering economic growth, cultural diffusion, and technological advancements, the extent of these effects varied, with some regions experiencing profound changes while others maintained traditional practices. (Summarizes the main argument.)

Explanation of Parts

  • Context: Provides background information to situate the argument in its historical setting.
  • Thesis Statement: A clear, defensible claim that responds to the prompt and establishes a line of reasoning.
  • Line of Reasoning: Outlines how the argument will be structured in the essay.
  • Topic Sentences: Each body paragraph supports a specific part of the thesis.
  • Evidence: Uses specific historical examples to support the argument.
  • Analysis: Explains the significance of the evidence and how it supports the thesis.
  • Restating the Thesis: Concludes by summarizing the argument and reinforcing the thesis.
